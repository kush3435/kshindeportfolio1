Design of Load Stability System

• Developed a Load Stability System (LSS) that deploys two electric duct fans to achieve bi-directional thrust on an autonomous suspended load control platform using a single motor.

• Managed all phases of the design process for 60% of the components, assembly and sub-assemblies, including drafting, dimensioning, GD&T, tolerance stack up analysis, prototyping and documenting results.

• Worked with Project Manager, Financial coordinator and manufacturing shop to ensure on-time delivery for project.

• Performed FMEA, preliminary CFD to determine fan to fan distance and designed experiments for testing bearings for endurance.

• Manufactured and assembled a complete functioning model with all the required specifications including alignment, thrust to weight ratio, efficiency, bi-directional thrusts and ramp up/down time.

Automated Design of a Gripper using Topology Optimization

Traditional design approaches don’t make the most of new manufacturing methods, like additive manufacturing, which are removing design constraints and opening up new possibilities. The optimal shape of a part is often organic and counterintuitive, so designing it requires a different approach. Topology optimization lets you specify where supports and loads are located on a volume of material and lets the software find the best shape.

• In this project, Topology Optimization Algorithm is used which consists of a Mesher, FEA module and optimizer on MATLAB to automatically generate an optimal structure for a Gripper subjected to boundary conditions such as volume constraints and load conditions with an objective of minimizing deflection.

• This design was verified using FEA on SolidWorks.

Shape memory alloy review

Structural and functional Fatigue is a key issue that needs to be resolved in order to promote the engineering applications of Shape Memory Alloys(SMA).

In this paper, recent progresses made in theoretical and experimental analyses regarding structural fatigue features of Shape Memory alloys especially Nitinol (Nickel-Titanium Alloy) are reviewed.

Further, the applications of SMAs are reviewed; finally, summary and future topics are outlined.

Kanban Implementation

Kanban is a visual method for controlling production as part of Just in Time (JIT) and Lean Manufacturing. As part of a pull system it controls what is produced, in what quantity, and when. Its purpose is to ensure that you only produce what the customer is asking for and nothing more.

In this project, Kanban was implemented for 18 product variants and other Lean six sigma strategies such as Cellular manufacturing, Poka Yoke, etc. on production floor.

Increased productivity by 5.6%, area savings by 43.47% and potential profit by more than $20000.
